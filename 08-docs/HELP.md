# Using Successor

## What this is
Successor helps a business owner get the knowledge that lives only in
their head into documents a successor, a buyer, or their family can
actually use. Everything stays on your computer. Nothing is sent anywhere.

## The shape of it
1. Start a project - the business name and your name are all it needs.
2. Sit for interview sessions - eight parts, in any order, one unhurried
   sitting at a time. Answer in your own words; everything is saved
   exactly as you say it. Anything left hanging is remembered and asked
   again next time.
3. Add documents - vendor lists, old procedures, notes (plain text: .txt,
   .md, or .csv today). If a document disagrees with something you said,
   you will see both and choose.
4. Watch the dashboard - completeness, risks, open questions, freshness.
5. Generate the succession package - nine documents, all in your words,
   marked wherever something still needs your confirmation.

## Keeping your own copy
"Export this project" saves a single file. Keep it with your important
papers, or move it to another computer with "Restore from a project file."

## Deleting a project from this computer
"Delete from this computer…" on a project's card removes the working copy
and its automatic backup here. It cannot be undone, and it does not touch
files you have exported or reports you have printed. The app will ask you
to confirm that you have an exported copy and have checked that it
restores - do that first, every time. (If this computer is used to prepare
projects for someone else, see CUSTODY.md for the full hand-over routine.)

## Protecting this computer
"Protect this computer" (on the home screen) locks everything stored here
behind a passphrase of your choosing. The passphrase is never written down
anywhere - which also means **a forgotten passphrase cannot be recovered**,
by anyone. Your exported project files stay readable on purpose, so keep
one somewhere safe. Details in SECURITY.md.

## What Successor will never do
It will never invent an answer for you, never store a password or account
number, and never delete something you said - even when a newer answer
replaces it, the record keeps both and shows which one you confirmed.
